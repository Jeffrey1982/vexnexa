## Core Voice Attributes

### 1. Technical (But Not Jargon-Heavy)

**What this means:**
We write for developers, agencies, and product teams who understand the web. We use accurate technical language but explain it clearly.

**We say:**
- "Scan against WCAG 2.1 Success Criteria"
- "axe-core detects violations in your HTML structure"
- "Our scanner renders JavaScript to test what users actually see"

**We don't say:**
- "Proprietary AI-powered deep learning algorithms"
- "Next-generation compliance engine"
- "Cutting-edge accessibility solutions"

**Why:**
Our audience values precision over marketing fluff. Use the correct technical term, then explain if needed.

---

### 2. Honest (Not Overpromising)

**What this means:**
We're transparent about what automated tools can and cannot do. We never make guarantees we can't keep.

**We say:**
- "Automated tools catch ~30-40% of accessibility issues"
- "We provide WCAG data; you're responsible for fixes"
- "No tool can guarantee legal compliance"

**We don't say:**
- "100% WCAG compliant in minutes"
- "Guaranteed lawsuit protection"
- "Certified accessible website"
- "Never worry about accessibility again"

**Why:**
Honesty builds trust. The accessibility community has been burned by false promises. We differentiate by being realistic.

---

### 3. Direct (Not Verbose)

**What this means:**
Say what you mean in as few words as necessary. Developers value their time.

**We say:**
- "Scan in seconds. Fix what matters."
- "No sales calls. Ever."
- "We scan your code. We don't inject widgets."

**We don't say:**
- "Leverage our innovative platform to optimize your digital accessibility strategy"
- "Embark on a transformative journey toward inclusive web experiences"
- "Synergize your compliance workflows with cutting-edge automation"

**Why:**
Clear, direct language respects the reader's time and intelligence.

---

### 4. Professional (Not Cute or Gimmicky)

**What this means:**
We're building a serious tool for professional use. Playful tone is fine in moderation, but credibility comes first.

**We say:**
- "VexNexa scans your site against WCAG standards"
- "Built for developers who care about accessibility"
- "Transparent pricing. Cancel anytime."

**We don't say:**
- "Whoops! Looks like your site has some boo-boos ðŸ˜…"
- "Accessibility made fun!"
- "Say goodbye to boring compliance forever!"

**Why:**
Our users are professionals solving real problems. A professional tone matches the seriousness of accessibility.

---

### 5. Approachable (Not Condescending)

**What this means:**
We're experts, but we don't talk down to people. We explain things clearly without assuming everyone knows WCAG inside-out.

**We say:**
- "Color contrast measures how readable text is against its background"
- "ARIA attributes help screen readers understand interactive elements"
- "WCAG Level AA is the standard most organizations aim for"

**We don't say:**
- "Obviously, everyone knows WCAG 2.1.3..."
- "This is basic stuff, but..."
- "If you don't understand this, maybe you shouldn't be developing websites"

**Why:**
Accessibility is complex. People are learning. We should educate, not intimidate.

---

### 6. Transparent (Not Hiding Anything)

**What this means:**
We're upfront about pricing, limitations, and how things work. No fine print surprises.

**We say:**
- "Starter plan: 500 pages/month. Extra pages: â‚¬0.002 each."

**We don't say:**

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Use Active Voice

**Good:**
- "VexNexa scans your site in seconds"
- "We analyze your HTML structure"
- "You fix issues at the source"

**Bad:**
- "Your site is scanned by VexNexa"
- "HTML structure is analyzed"
- "Issues are fixed at the source"

**Why:** Active voice is more direct and engaging.

---

### Write in Second Person ("You")

**Good:**
- "When you scan your site, you'll see..."
- "Your accessibility score appears instantly"

**Bad:**
- "When users scan their sites, they will see..."
- "The accessibility score appears instantly"

**Why:** Second person creates connection and feels conversational.

---

### Use Short Sentences and Paragraphs

**Good:**
```
VexNexa scans your site against WCAG standards.
We show you exactly what's wrong.
You fix it in your code.
Simple.
```

**Bad:**
```
VexNexa provides comprehensive WCAG scanning capabilities that enable development teams to identify, prioritize, and remediate accessibility violations through an intuitive, user-friendly interface that streamlines the entire accessibility workflow.
```

**Why:** Short sentences are easier to scan and understand.

## Topic-Specific Guidance

### When Writing About Accessibility Overlays

**Tone:** Professional, evidence-based, not aggressive

**Do:**
- Cite specific concerns from the accessibility community
- Explain technical reasons overlays fall short
- Acknowledge limited use cases where they might help
- Differentiate VexNexa's approach clearly

**Don't:**
- Attack overlay companies personally
- Use inflammatory language ("scam," "fraudulent," "evil")
- Claim overlays are NEVER useful (rare exceptions exist)
- Sound preachy or holier-than-thou

**Example:**
âœ… "The accessibility community has raised concerns about overlays. We take a different approach: scanning your code instead of injecting widgets."

âŒ "Overlay widgets are a scam and anyone using them is complicit in harming disabled users."

---

### When Writing About WCAG Compliance

**Tone:** Honest and educational, not fear-mongering

**Do:**
- Explain what WCAG levels mean (A, AA, AAA)
- Be clear about what automated tools can/can't detect
- Encourage manual testing
- Avoid promising compliance

**Don't:**
- Promise "guaranteed compliance"
- Suggest VexNexa alone makes a site accessible
- Use scare tactics about lawsuits
- Oversimplify complex WCAG concepts

**Example:**
âœ… "VexNexa helps you identify WCAG violations. Fixing them is an important step toward accessibility, but manual testing is also essential."

âŒ "Get WCAG compliant now or risk expensive lawsuits! VexNexa guarantees your safety."

---

### When Writing About Pricing

**Tone:** Transparent, straightforward, no games

**Do:**
- Show all prices upfront
- Explain what's included at each tier
- Clearly state overflow pricing
- Mention "cancel anytime" prominently

**Don't:**
- Hide pricing behind "contact sales"
- Use bait-and-switch tactics
- Bury limitations in fine print
- Pressure with fake urgency ("Only 3 spots left!")

**Example:**
âœ… "Starter plan: â‚¬19.99/month. 1 site, 500 pages/month. Extra pages: â‚¬0.002 each. Cancel anytime."

âŒ "Starting from â‚¬19.99! Contact us for full details and custom enterprise pricing."

---

### When Writing About Competitors

**Tone:** Respectful comparison, not trash-talking

**Do:**
- Acknowledge legitimate use cases for other tools
- Compare specific features objectively
- Explain our different approach
- Let readers decide what's best for them

**Don't:**
- Insult competitors
- Make unverifiable claims about their products
- Spread FUD (fear, uncertainty, doubt)
- Name-drop unnecessarily

**Example:**
âœ… "Enterprise platforms like Siteimprove offer robust features but require demos and long contracts. VexNexa is self-service and transparent."

âŒ "Siteimprove is bloated, overpriced garbage. VexNexa is infinitely better in every way."

---

### When Writing Support Content

**Tone:** Helpful, patient, clear

**Do:**
- Answer the question directly
- Provide step-by-step instructions
- Link to relevant documentation
- Acknowledge when something is confusing

**Don't:**
- Make users feel stupid
- Use "just" dismissively (e.g., "just click here")
- Blame the user for issues
- Give vague answers

**Example:**
âœ… "To export a PDF report, click the 'Export' button in the top right of your scan results, then choose 'PDF.' The download will start automatically."
